Comal — a voice-first transcription app

I built a small app called Comal: hold down the spacebar, speak, and get back a clean card with your words transcribed.

The part I like most is not just the transcription. It is the rhythm of the interaction. You speak once, the app shows that it is Transcribing…, then Polishing…, and finally returns something that feels more like usable writing than raw audio exhaust.

Under the hood, Comal uses Sarvam for speech-to-text, with the transcription flowing through a server-side route so the API key never touches the browser. Each recording becomes its own little artifact instead of disappearing into one giant text blob.

Demo: speak a sentence, watch Comal transcribe and polish it into readable text.

This kind of project scratches a very particular itch for me. There is something satisfying about shrinking the distance between speech and finished text — especially when the interface gets out of the way and lets the flow feel obvious.

Comal is built with Next.js and React, and the current setup is intentionally simple: record, transcribe, return a card. Small surface area, clear loop, useful result.
